01 — Brand Story

A gift, freely given.

寄贈
KIZŌ
a gift — a donation — to present something freely

Kizo (寄贈, kizō) means to give something as a gift — to present it freely, with no expectation of return. That's the entire philosophy in a single word. Kizo is free and open-source software, built for small business owners, given away because they deserve tools as good as what the Fortune 500 has — and shouldn't have to pay a SaaS tax to get them.

Every restaurant owner paying $300/month for a POS. Every salon owner juggling five disconnected apps. Every mechanic doing books at midnight because the software costs more than it saves. Kizo exists because those people got a raw deal, and because for the first time in history, one person with AI can build what used to take a team of fifty.

So we built it. And we're giving it away.

The name was found, not invented — a happy typo during a late-night working session. The best gifts arrive like that: unplanned, unexpected, and immediately right.

"You make the food. You cut the hair. You fix the car. We built the operating system. It's yours. Take it."

Brand values

Free means free
Not freemium. Not free-until-we-raise-prices. Not free-but-we-sell-your-data. Free and open-source, forever. The code is yours to read, run, modify, and share.
Make it simple
If the owner needs a manual, we failed. Every screen, every notification, every report should be obvious in three seconds. Open-source doesn't have to mean ugly or complicated.
Smart, not clever
We use AI to save time and money — but we never make the owner feel stupid. The intelligence is in the background. The simplicity is in the foreground.
Your business, your data
Local-first. Your data lives on your hardware. No cloud lock-in. No extraction. No selling your customers to your competitors. You own everything, because it was always yours.
ORIGIN STORY
Kizo was born from a restaurant kitchen. Its creator spent 20 years running a Thai café in Washington State while building software for Fortune 500 companies — and watched as the tools available to small businesses got worse and more expensive every year. When AI coding agents made it possible for one person to build what once took a team, he built the operating system his own restaurant always needed — a POS, an ordering platform, a delivery system, an analytics engine — in days instead of months. Then he gave it away. Because the best tools shouldn't be locked behind a monthly fee.
The open-source commitment
Kizo is licensed under a permissive open-source license. The entire codebase — POS, ordering, inventory, accounting, analytics — is available for anyone to inspect, modify, and deploy. There is no "community edition" vs. "enterprise edition." There is no feature gating. There is no phone-home telemetry. The software is the gift. The business model, if there ever is one, will be services — setup help, custom development, managed hosting — never the software itself.
02 — Logo

Simply given.

The Kizo mark is three elements: two hands and a gift. Nothing else. Two open hands reach toward a warm orb — something precious, offered freely. It's the most reduced expression of the brand's core idea: here, take this, it's yours.

The warm circle is the software itself — the thing we built and are giving away. The hands are the act of giving — open, unhesitating, no strings attached.

Clear space & usage rules
Maintain clear space equal to the diameter of the gift circle on all sides. Never rotate, stretch, recolor the gift circle with non-brand colors, or place on busy backgrounds. The mark and wordmark should never be separated in the primary lockup. The mark and app icon may be used independently where the brand is already established.

05 — Brand Voice

Talk like a neighbor, not a vendor.

Kizo speaks in first person plural — "we." We never say "the platform" or "the system." We talk like a person who happens to be really good at running the back office. Warm, clear, honest. We celebrate the owner's craft, never our own cleverness.

NOT THIS
Our automated reconciliation engine processes multi-channel settlement data in real-time, providing actionable financial insights.
THIS
We match every sale to every deposit, every day. If something doesn't add up, we'll tell you exactly what's off.
NOT THIS
Error 4012: Payment gateway connection timeout. Please verify your merchant account credentials and retry.
THIS
The card reader lost its connection — this usually fixes itself in about 30 seconds. If not, try turning it off and back on.
NOT THIS
Leverage our advanced customer retention analytics to identify at-risk segments and deploy targeted re-engagement campaigns.
THIS
Sarah hasn't been in for 6 weeks — she usually comes every two. Want to send her a welcome-back offer?
NOT THIS
Optimize your labor allocation with AI-driven demand forecasting and predictive scheduling algorithms.
THIS
Based on the last few months, Thursday nights need one more person in the kitchen. Want to adjust the schedule?
Voice principles
Say "we", not "the system"We noticed your food cost went up 3% this week.
Name the person, not the segmentMrs. Johnson's gift card has $12 left.
Tell them what to do, not what happenedThe printer needs reconnecting — tap here to fix it.
Celebrate their craft142 five-star reviews this year. Your customers love what you do.
Be honest about limitsWe can't tell exactly why — but here are the three most likely reasons.
06 — Product Language

Every feature has a human name.

Kizo product names avoid jargon. Features are named for what they do in the owner's world. The pattern: Kizo + a single, common noun. If you have to explain the name, pick a different name.

Kizo RegisterThe point of sale — ring up customers, split checks, handle tips, print receipts.
Kizo OrdersOnline ordering, delivery management, and marketplace sync — all in one place.
Kizo BooksAccounting, reconciliation, and financial reporting. Where the money picture lives.
Kizo TeamScheduling, time tracking, payroll prep, and labor cost visibility.
Kizo StockInventory tracking, reorder alerts, vendor management, waste monitoring.
Kizo GuestsCustomer profiles, loyalty, reviews, and the marketing that brings people back.
Kizo PulseThe daily briefing — what happened yesterday, what matters today, what needs attention.
Kizo HelpSupport, troubleshooting, and the person you call when something breaks.
The Daily Briefing — "Kizo Pulse"
The signature product moment. Every morning, when the owner opens Kizo, they see Pulse: yesterday's sales, today's schedule, inventory alerts, new reviews, cash position, and anything that needs a decision. The loading animation is the Kizo mark — two hands offering the warm orb, the gift arriving for another day.
Naming conventions
Product names are always "Kizo" + a single, common English noun. Never an acronym. Never a portmanteau. Never a word the owner wouldn't use at their own dinner table. If you have to explain the name, pick a different name.
